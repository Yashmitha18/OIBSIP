A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

APJ Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India," he made significant contributions to the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. His expertise and leadership played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and the operationalization of strategic missile systems.Throughout his career, Abdul Kalam displayed unwavering dedication to education and inspiring young minds. As a teacher, he nurtured and mentored students, emphasizing the importance of knowledge, curiosity, and innovation. Kalam firmly believed in the power of technology and education to transform India into a developed nation.
